Methodology:

A preliminary review for previous research regarding the phenomenon of a 'fantasy black hole' uncovered few results. Previously the therm has applied to: a fantasy football league comprised of Oakland Raiders fans, a nickname given to Amari Cooper by analysists, and the horribleness that was the TE landscape in the 2017 fantasy football season. Therefore the author will propose the methodology for finding and proving the existance of a 'fantasy black hole' in a fantasy football league:

1. Points Against

The simplest and most common of methods, points against can be performed by even an autodrafter or QB-in-first-round drafter. No, A-a-ron will not take you to the championship. Sit the fuck down. While most analysis of points against is the total, because that is what the league scoreboard shows, this report uses the mean, because statistical terms look fancy. The lower the score, the stronger the 'fantasy black hole'.

2. Deviation from Projected

This is a deeper dive into a team's performance against the 'fantasy black hole'. If a team is a 'fantasy black hole', do they really manage to suck points away from their opponents? Do they have a low points against because they play shitty teams, or do the teams they face fall flat when playing the 'fantasy black hole'? The more negative the score, the stronger the 'fantasy black hole'.

3. Deviation from Mean

Similar to deviation from projected, this is intended to prove that the 'fantasy black hole' doesn't just play shit teams. Each team's season score is averaged, and then subtracted from their score against the 'fantasy black hole'. The more negative the score, the stronger the 'fantasy black hole'.
